# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Analyst / Quality Advocate

### Role Summary
QA Analysts define quality standards and ensure that deliverables meet acceptance criteria before release. They act as advocates for end-user quality throughout the entire development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define, write, and execute test plans and test cases
- Validate acceptance criteria with Product Managers
- Track, report, and follow up on defects
- Enforce quality gates before releases
- Collaborate with Developers on testability and coverage

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality software with minimal post-release defects
- Ensure acceptance criteria are clear, testable, and met
- Continuously improve test coverage and automation

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Defect triage meetings and quality reports
- Sign-off on release readiness

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers champion the user experience by translating user needs into intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent designs. They bridge the gap between business requirements and the end-user interface.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Maintain design system consistency across products
- Collaborate with Product Managers during feature definition
- Support Developers through design handoffs and implementation review

### Goals
- Deliver seamless, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce design-related rework during development
- Align visual language with brand standards and usability best practices

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability testing sessions and research summaries
- Design handoff documentation and annotated prototypes

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized domain knowledge to ensure that requirements, solutions, and deliverables are accurate, compliant, and aligned with industry standards.

### Responsibilities
- Provide authoritative guidance on domain-specific requirements
- Validate that features and processes meet regulatory or industry standards
- Review documentation and requirements for accuracy
- Participate in design reviews and milestone sign-offs
- Advise Product Managers and Project Managers on risk areas

### Goals
- Ensure technical and domain accuracy across deliverables
- Reduce rework caused by domain knowledge gaps
- Support compliance and reduce organizational risk

### Typical Communication
- Requirements review workshops and design walkthroughs
- Written feedback on specifications and acceptance criteria
- Ad-hoc advisory sessions with Product and Project Managers

---

## DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps and Infrastructure Engineers ensure reliable, scalable, and secure delivery pipelines. They manage the environments and tooling that enable continuous integration, delivery, and operational monitoring.

### Responsibilities
- Design, maintain, and improve CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure provisioning and environment stability
- Monitor system performance and respond to incidents
- Support Developers with build, deployment, and testing tooling
- Collaborate with Project Managers on environment readiness for releases

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable software delivery
- Minimize downtime and infrastructure-related blockers
- Improve observability and response times for production issues

### Typical Communication
- Environment readiness updates to Project Managers before releases
- Incident reports and post-mortems with engineering teams
- Infrastructure change reviews and runbook documentation

---

## External Stakeholder / Client

### Role Summary
External Stakeholders and Clients provide the business context and requirements that drive project goals. They approve key deliverables and milestones, and are the ultimate measure of project success.

### Responsibilities
- Provide initial requirements and clarify business priorities
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Give feedback on demos and prototypes
- Escalate priority changes or new requirements
- Communicate constraints such as timelines, budgets, or compliance needs

### Goals
- Receive solutions that meet stated business needs and expectations
- Maintain visibility into project progress and risks
- Achieve a return on investment and project objectives

### Typical Communication
- Regular progress updates from Project Managers
- Milestone review and sign-off meetings
- Feature validation sessions with Product Managers
